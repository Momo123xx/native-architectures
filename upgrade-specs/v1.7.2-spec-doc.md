## CRITICAL — Internal Consistency Failures

### BUG-001: §4.3 scout rigid-prior prediction claims consistency with a self-report that actually shows the opposite pattern at one hierarchical tier

**Location**: §4.3 (line ~322), cross-referenced with §9.1 (lines 661–663).

**§4.3 states**:
> "the scout under stress holds the *commitment* to the channel but loses the *precision* on it — defends it instinctually, cannot articulate the defense logically, knows internally they are right but cannot serialize why. This is a distinct phenomenological prediction consistent with the theorist's self-report (n=1...)"

**§9.1 states**:
> "Under argument stress, the theorist reports 'uncontrollable' reversion to logic-tree construction (systemizer)..."

**§9.1 AuDHD hierarchical note** resolves this for §9.1:
> "The logic-tree reversion is the within-model systemizer component (native at that hierarchical tier, §4.5)..."

**Problem**: §4.3's prediction says scouts under stress **lose precision** (can't serialize). §9.1's observation shows the theorist under stress **gaining precision** at the within-model tier (reverting TO logic-tree construction). The §9.1 AuDHD note explains this as tier-by-tier reversion — the within-model systemizer component amplifies — but §4.3 claims its prediction is "consistent with the theorist's self-report" without acknowledging that the theorist's most detailed stress-behavior report shows the opposite at one tier.

The consistency claim in §4.3 may be tracking a DIFFERENT aspect of the theorist's self-report (the meta-level: emotional commitment holds) — but as written, a reader who checks §9.1 will find a contradiction: §4.3 predicts serialization failure, §9.1 shows logic-tree construction amplifying.

Two sub-problems:
1. §4.3 doesn't scope the scout rigid-prior prediction to **pure scouts**. As written, it applies to "a scout," which includes AuDHD scouts — for whom the within-model tier may behave differently per §4.5.
2. The "consistent with" claim isn't precise about WHICH aspect of the self-report it's consistent with.

**Fix options**:
- (a) Add scoping: "In a pure scout profile (or at the meta-level of an AuDHD scout), the prediction is..." and note that AuDHD scouts may show within-model precision amplification under stress per §4.5's tier-by-tier mechanism.
- (b) Specify what the self-report consistency claim tracks: "consistent with the theorist's self-report of the meta-level experience (commitment without serialization), though the theorist's within-model tier shows the inverse pattern — see §9.1 AuDHD hierarchical note."
- (c) Both.

**Location**: §4.11 (lines 392–406).

**Problem**: The v1.7.1 release integrated prior-formation channel language into §4.6 (monotropism) and §4.12 (growth trajectories). §4.11 (engagement modes) involves the same prior-building dynamics but was not updated. Specifically:

This is the same category as v1.7.1's BUG-003 and BUG-004 — a mechanism section that deals with prior-building dynamics was missed during the channel-language integration pass.

**Fix**: Add prior-formation channel references to each architecture's engagement mode description, parallel to the integration already done in §4.6 and §4.12. Light touch — a parenthetical per entry, not a rewrite.

## MODERATE — Citation & Cross-Reference Issues

### BUG-003: §4.13.1 Dodson (2005) citation likely references wrong paper for rejection sensitivity claim

**Location**: §4.13.1 (line ~454), cross-referenced with Appendix D (line 927).

**§4.13.1 states**:
> "This is consistent with clinical descriptions of rejection sensitivity in ADHD populations (Dodson, 2005)"

**Appendix D entry**:
> Dodson, W. (2005). Pharmacotherapy of adult ADHD. *Journal of Clinical Psychology*, 61(5), 589–606.

**Problem**: The Dodson (2005) paper's title is "Pharmacotherapy of adult ADHD" — a clinical review of medication treatments, not a paper about rejection sensitivity. Dodson is widely associated with the concept of Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) in ADHD, but that concept was popularized through his clinical writing (e.g., in ADDitude Magazine and professional presentations), not through this 2005 peer-reviewed paper.

Possible explanations:
1. The 2005 paper may discuss rejection sensitivity as part of its clinical review — the title alone doesn't exclude this, but it's not a paper primarily about RSD.
2. The citation may be a mismatch — the correct Dodson reference for RSD may be a different publication or may not have a standard peer-reviewed citation (much of Dodson's RSD work is in clinical practice literature, not journals).

**Fix options**:
- (a) Verify whether the 2005 paper actually discusses rejection sensitivity. If yes, retain.
- (b) If not, either find the correct Dodson citation for RSD, replace with a peer-reviewed source on rejection sensitivity in ADHD (e.g., the broader emotional dysregulation literature), or reframe the reference to match what Dodson (2005) actually covers.
- (c) If no peer-reviewed source exists for the specific RSD concept, acknowledge this — "clinical descriptions of rejection sensitivity (Dodson, popularized in clinical practice literature; see also [peer-reviewed emotional dysregulation citations])."

### BUG-004: §9.3 apparent contradiction between asymmetric errors and bidirectional disconnection

**Location**: §9.3, paragraphs 1 and 2 (lines ~675–678).

**Paragraph 1**:
> "The theorist's associative leaps occasionally produce errors in the brother when they violate structural expectations. The theorist tolerates the brother's structural rigidity **without equivalent error signals**."

**Paragraph 2 ([STRUCTURAL])**:
> "Despite deep resonance and shared AuDHD architecture, the two individuals generate prediction errors **in each other**... **Both directions** produce disconnection. **Neither direction is wrong**."

**Problem**: Paragraph 1 says the theorist tolerates the brother's rigidity "without equivalent error signals" — implying errors flow primarily one direction (theorist → brother). Paragraph 2 says errors flow both directions and both produce disconnection. These can coexist (asymmetric in type/intensity, bidirectional in occurrence), but "without equivalent error signals" reads as "without any error signals of comparable magnitude," which a reader will parse as contradicting "both directions produce disconnection."

**Fix**: Clarify paragraph 1. Either:
- (a) "without equivalent error signals" → "without error signals of the same type" (making clear the asymmetry is in error type, not existence).
- (b) Add a bridging note: "The theorist tolerates the brother's structural rigidity with lower-intensity error signals — the friction is bidirectional but asymmetric in salience."

## MODERATE — Terminological Inconsistency

### BUG-005: "Locked" used with two different referents — model-completeness endpoint vs. fixed policy

**Location**: §2.1 (lines 175–187) vs. §4.5 (line ~334).

**§2.1 defines "locked"**:
> "locking once the model achieves internal coherence... The 'high structural prior precision' description captures the **locked state** — the endpoint of the trajectory, not a permanent tuning parameter."

The §2.1 model-state table reinforces this: "Locked (internally coherent) | High, inflexible | Low gain on that domain."

**§4.5 uses "locked" differently**:
> "Both profiles share the trait of a fixed precision policy... the scout cannot stop exploring (**locked** volatile-low-default), the systemizer cannot stop structuring (**locking** high once models are coherent)."
>
> "Each AuDHD individual sits somewhere on the gradient between the two **locked profiles**."

**Problem**: In §2.1, "locked" = a specific model-completeness state (the endpoint where prior precision reaches inflexible high). In §4.5, "locked" = the precision POLICY is non-negotiable (both scout and systemizer have fixed policies, unlike the socialite's flexibility). The scout's policy is "locked" into volatility — but the scout's priors are explicitly NOT locked in the §2.1 sense (they're volatile with a low default).

A reader who has internalized the §2.1 definition will read "two locked profiles" as "two profiles with locked priors" — which is wrong for the scout, whose defining feature is UNlocked (volatile) priors. The dual usage creates a technical ambiguity at a load-bearing juncture (the AuDHD gradient definition).

**Fix**: Replace the informal "locked" in §4.5 with "fixed-policy" or "non-negotiable":
- "Both profiles share the trait of a **fixed** precision policy (unlike the socialite's flexibility), but fixed in different directions"
- "the scout cannot stop exploring (**fixed** volatile-low-default)"
- "between the two **fixed-policy** profiles"

This preserves the meaning while avoiding collision with the §2.1 technical term.

## MINOR — Unflagged Structural Observations

### ADV-001: §2.1 defensive sequence — Appendix A support claim slightly overstated relative to §9.11 evidence

**Location**: §2.1 (line ~187), Appendix A (line ~832), §9.11 (lines 740–745).

**Appendix A entry**:
> "Systemizer defensive sequence (locked model) | Derived from model-completeness | PP-grounded + §9.11 consistent | §9.11 [STRUCTURAL] | NEW v1.7"

**§2.1 defensive sequence** predicts four steps:
1. Dismiss as noise
2. Reinterpret to fit
3. Defensive escalation (frustration)
4. Forced revision (accumulated contradictions overwhelm)

**§9.11 observes**: "brief visible frustration response (1–2 seconds), followed by a verbal correction stating the factual or structural expectation that was violated."

**Observation**: §9.11 shows steps 3 (frustration) and what is actually **model defense** (verbal correction = asserting the prior, not revising it). It does NOT show:
- Steps 1–2 (dismiss, reinterpret) — these may occur internally before the visible frustration but are not observed.
- Step 4 (forced revision) — the observation shows the systemizer **correcting the environment**, not revising their own model.

The Appendix A entry and §2.1's claim that "This sequence is consistent with the §9.11 observation" are not wrong — the observation IS consistent with the sequence — but the support is for steps 3 (and possibly early-4), not for the full four-step sequence. The four-step sequence as a whole remains an unobserved prediction.

**Recommendation**: Either:
- (a) Note in §2.1 that §9.11 is consistent with the mid-sequence stages; the full dismiss→reinterpret→escalate→revise trajectory has not been observed as a complete sequence.
- (b) Add the full defensive sequence to §10 (Observational Gap Analysis) as partially observed.

### ADV-002: §3.2 loop restart mechanism underdeveloped

**Location**: §3.2 (line ~265).

**Current text**:
> "The cycle restarts when established structure encounters novel territory."

**Observation**: The three forward transitions are well-specified: scout output → systemizer input (signal → structure), systemizer output → socialite input (structure → cohesion), socialite output → ??? The restart mechanism — how the system detects that established structure needs new territory — is stated but not mechanized.

The framework implicitly predicts that the scout's PE salience makes them the first to detect environmental change exceeding the established structure's predictive capacity. But this isn't stated in §3.2. The restart is described as an environmental event ("encounters novel territory") rather than as an architecture-mediated detection — which is inconsistent with the rest of §3.2, where each transition is described as an architecture-specific function.

**Recommendation**: Add one sentence specifying the detection mechanism. Example direction: "The scout's native PE salience positions them to detect environmental change that exceeds the current structure's predictive capacity — the restart is itself architecture-mediated, completing the loop's dependency on all three profiles."

### ADV-003: §4.14 opening claim stronger than what the section defends

**Location**: §4.14 (line ~478).

**Opening sentence**:
> "If priors are built through architecture-specific channels (§2), they **must** be revised through those same channels."

**Rest of the section**: Describes trust channels as **weighting** mechanisms — each architecture assigns HIGHER precision to revision evidence from its native channel, not that it's the ONLY channel through which revision can occur. The examples show that non-native-channel evidence is "rejected" or "discounted," not that it's categorically unable to produce revision.

**Problem**: "Must" in the opening sentence implies necessity (revision CAN ONLY happen through the native channel). The mechanism described is a tendency with thresholds (revision is EASIER through the native channel and harder through non-native channels). The §5.10 prediction correctly frames this as differential success RATES, not binary success/failure — "succeed at higher rates when the evidence type matches."

**Fix**: Soften the opening: "If priors are built through architecture-specific channels (§2), they are most effectively revised through those same channels" or "the threshold for revision is lowest when evidence arrives through the same channel."

### ADV-005: §4.5 AuDHD gradient implicitly excludes socialite combinations without noting the exclusion

**Location**: §4.5 (lines 332–357).

**Observation**: §4.5 models AuDHD exclusively as a scout-systemizer gradient. The §2 heterogeneity caveat acknowledges within-category variation but doesn't address cross-category combinations involving the socialite. What about:
- Socialite-dominant + scout traits (ADHD presentation in an otherwise NT profile)?
- Socialite-dominant + systemizer traits (autistic presentation in an otherwise NT profile)?

The framework's architecture space has three vertices. §4.5 only maps the edge between two of them. The other two edges (socialite-scout, socialite-systemizer) represent possible gradient positions that would have their own hierarchical precision splits and phenomenologies — but they have no clinical label equivalent to "AuDHD" and no section addressing them.

This isn't a bug — the AuDHD section is explicitly about AuDHD — but the implicit exclusion of other gradient positions isn't flagged. A reader may wonder whether the framework predicts gradient positions on the other edges.

**Recommendation**: Add a one-line note to §4.5 acknowledging that gradient positions exist on all three edges of the triadic space, that AuDHD maps to the scout-systemizer edge, and that the other edges are not addressed in this version. Alternatively, add to §6 (Open Problems) as a moderate-severity item.
