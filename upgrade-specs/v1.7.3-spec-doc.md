## MODERATE — Terminological Residual

### BUG-001: §3.3 "two locked architectures" — missed in v1.7.2 BUG-005 fix

**Location**: §3.3 (line ~303).

**Current text**:
> "The two **locked** architectures map to positions in this space: high-prior + high-PE (systemizer) and volatile-prior (low default) + PE-salience-as-consequence-of-default (scout)."

**Problem**: The v1.7.2 BUG-005 fix changed §4.5's informal "locked" to "fixed-policy" to avoid collision with §2.1's technical definition of "locked" (model-completeness endpoint). §3.3 uses "locked" in the same informal sense as the old §4.5 — "the two architectures that occupy fixed positions in the prior/PE space." But the scout is explicitly NOT locked in the §2.1 sense — the scout's defining feature is volatile (unlocked) priors. A reader who has internalized §2.1's definition will parse "two locked architectures" as "two architectures with locked priors," which is wrong for the scout.

This is the same class of ambiguity that BUG-005 was designed to eliminate, but §3.3 was missed in the fix pass.

**Fix**: "The two **fixed-policy** architectures map to positions in this space" — parallel to the §4.5 terminology.

**Location**: §6.1 (line ~600).

**Current text**:
> "**Unfalsifiability — partially addressed.** Three candidate falsification criteria exist. The boundary-absorption problem persists..."

**Problem**: The document claims three falsification criteria exist but does not state them anywhere in §6.1, and does not cross-reference where they can be found. A reader looking for the criteria — which are load-bearing for the framework's scientific credibility — cannot locate them from this text.

The changelog (line 866, Appendix B) says "Define falsification criteria. Partially addressed v1.1." This suggests the criteria were introduced in v1.1 but may have been present in a section that was later reorganized (the v1.4 structural reorganization, or the v1.5 "why three" reframing). If the criteria are implicit in §5's predictions (e.g., §5.1 stress-polarization, §5.8 clustering), they should be explicitly stated or cross-referenced.

**Fix options**:
- (a) State the three criteria in §6.1: "Three candidate falsification criteria exist: [criterion 1], [criterion 2], [criterion 3]."
- (b) Cross-reference: "Three candidate falsification criteria exist (see §5.1, §5.8, §[X])."
- (c) If the criteria cannot be recovered or were never explicitly stated, acknowledge this: "Falsification criteria were proposed in v1.1 but have not been explicitly enumerated in the current document. The strongest candidates are [...]."

## MINOR — Citation Mismatch Residual

### BUG-003: §4.13.1 Dodson in-text characterization doesn't match the cited paper

**Location**: §4.13.1 (line ~473).

**Current text**:
> "Dodson, 2005, on emotional components of adult ADHD; note: the specific 'rejection sensitive dysphoria' construct originates in Dodson's clinical practice work..."

**Appendix D entry**:
> Dodson, W. (2005). **Pharmacotherapy of adult ADHD**. *Journal of Clinical Psychology*, 61(5), 589–606.

**Problem**: The v1.7.2 BUG-003 fix correctly added the RSD clinical-practice caveat, but the in-text characterization still describes the paper as being "on emotional components of adult ADHD." The paper's actual title is "Pharmacotherapy of adult ADHD" — a clinical pharmacotherapy review, not a paper about emotional components. The "emotional components" framing implies the paper's primary topic is ADHD emotional processing, which overstates what a pharmacotherapy review covers.

**Fix**: Drop the characterization: "Dodson, 2005; note: the 'rejection sensitive dysphoria' construct originates in Dodson's clinical practice work and lacks peer-reviewed validation as a distinct mechanism." Or: "Dodson, 2005, a pharmacotherapy review that discusses emotional aspects of adult ADHD in a clinical context; note: the specific 'rejection sensitive dysphoria' construct..."

## MINOR — Nonstandard Terminology

### BUG-005: "Autonomicity" used consistently where standard term is "automaticity"

**Locations**: §4.3 (line ~335), §5.1 (line ~525), §10 (line ~790), Appendix A (line ~855).

**Current usage**: "autonomicity depth" — defined as "the degree to which a capacity runs automatically without top-down maintenance."

**Problem**: The standard term in cognitive science for the degree to which a process runs automatically is **automaticity** (from "automatic"), not "autonomicity" (which reads as derived from "autonomy" — self-governance, not automatic processing). The document uses "autonomicity" consistently (4 occurrences), so this is not a typo but a word-choice pattern. However, a reader with cognitive science background will find it nonstandard and potentially confusing — "autonomicity" suggests independence of action, not automatization of processing.

**Fix**: Replace "autonomicity" with "automaticity" throughout:
- "automaticity depth" (§4.3)
- "order of automaticity depth" (§5.1)
- "Automaticity-depth shedding order" (§10)
- "shedding tracks automaticity depth" (Appendix A)

## MINOR — Undefined Term

### BUG-006: §9.4 "AuDHD/2e" — "2e" appears once without definition

**Location**: §9.4 (line ~703).

**Current text**:
> "three proposed AuDHD/2e individuals (theorist, brother, father)"

**Problem**: "2e" (twice-exceptional: gifted + neurodevelopmental condition) appears in this single instance without definition or connection to the framework. Additionally, the father's architecture attribution is imprecise — §9.4 describes him as expressing care "through systemizer channels (financial stability, practical problem-solving)" but groups him under "AuDHD/2e" without specifying whether he is proposed AuDHD (both scout and systemizer components) or pure systemizer. The brother (§9.3, line 692) has a confirmed ASD diagnosis; the father's status is unstated beyond the systemizer-channel observation.

**Fix options**:
- (a) Remove "2e" and specify: "three proposed non-socialite individuals" or "three individuals proposed as systemizer/scout-dominant."
- (b) If the father is specifically proposed AuDHD, state this. If the "2e" label is doing specific work (indicating giftedness + ND), define it briefly.
- (c) Simplest: "a household with three proposed non-socialite individuals (theorist, brother, father)" — this avoids introducing undefined clinical terminology while preserving the structural claim (the mother's socialite architecture was mismatched with a non-socialite-dominant household).
